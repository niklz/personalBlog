INTRODUCTION

This (series of) post(s) aims to give a basics introduction to Regular Expressions, covering the most important concepts, showing some fun use-cases, and hopefully inspiring others to use them in their own text-processing workflows. Specifically I will be using examples in the R environment.

Humans are adept at noticing patterns in many walks of life, with text strings being no exception. For instance the telephone numbers

01632123456

01632 123456

  1. 212 3456

  2. 123-456

read by a human are all easily parsed and understood to be the same number. To a computer however, these strings are all unique. The good news is that computers can be ‘taught’ to understand patterns.

Regular Expressions (RegEx for short) formalise the concept of a text pattern. Using RegEx in a data-wrangling workflow is very powerful. Pattern detection, extraction, and replacement are common tasks which are well suited to RegEx.
